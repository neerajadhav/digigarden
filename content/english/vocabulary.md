## C

### **Commensurate**

> adjective  
> corresponding in size, importance, quality, etc.  
> _Example: His salary would be commensurate with his experience_

---

## H

### **Heterogeneous**

> _adjective_  
> consisting of parts or elements that are different or diverse in character.  
> _Example: A heterogeneous network connects devices with different operating systems and hardware types._

---

## I

### **Intermittent**

> _adjective_  
> Stopping and Starting at intervals; periodic.  
> _Example: In India, network connectivity remains intermittent in rural areas._

---

## M

### **Manifest**

> _verb_  
> to show something or to be shown clearly  
> _Example: Physical layer manifests the essential cloud characteristics_

---

## P

### **Paradigm**

> _noun_  
> one that serves as a pattern or model  
> _Example: Cloud computing is a paradigm for enabling network access to computing resources._

### **Provisioned**

> _verb_  
> to supply a person (or thing) with provisions (necessary items) to replace those consumed.  
> _Example: Resources are provisioned in cloud computing to meet demands of services._

---

## U

### **Ubiquitous**

> _adjective_  
> seeming to be everywhere or in several places at the same time; very common  
> _Example: Cloud computing enables ubiquitous computer resources._

### **Unilaterally**

> _adverb_  
> done by one person, group, or country involved in a situation without the agreement of others.  
> _Example: The company unilaterally decided to terminate the contract without consulting stakeholders._
